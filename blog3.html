Next Step At Spotify

April 25, 2022

Background In 2006, Spotify was founded in Sweden as an online music streaming platform. It started in Sweden and some Western European countries in 2008 and launched in the United States in 2011. By the end of 2018, Spotify had service in 78 countries over the world and collaborated with Tencent, the Chinese online retail giant, to enter the market in China. By far, Spotify entered every major music streaming market in the world. In order to stay competitive in the streaming industry, Spotify bought three podcasting companies in early 2019 and attempted to produce original content of its own to attract customers.

The music industry has evolved dramatically from vinyl records and cassettes to CDs and to online streaming platforms in the past two decades. The music industry is composed of musicians (suppliers), labels (distributors), and streaming platforms(retailers). Right now, the distribution is dominated by three major companies (Sony, Warner, and Universal). The musicians will supply music to these distributors and online retailers like Spotify would provide streaming services to individual customers. Spotify's recent action on podcasting can help Spotify to connect with suppliers directly without paying fees to distributors. This new strategy would potentially help the company to lower the cost and make more profits. Because there are legal issues regarding the copyrights of the music. The performance rights are usually held by the label companies and were pricer (Vroom et al., 2019). Spotify needs to sign complex contracts with labels in order to steam the music to customers. If Spotify can directly own the rights of the music supplier, it would save the company a lot of money.

Spotify had consecutive losses for over ten years but it announced its operating profit for the first time after signing a new deal with major labels in 2017. Although Spotify is the largest streaming company in the industry, Spotify still faces tough challenges from other music streaming platforms such as Apple Music, Amazon Prime, and Google’s Youtube Music. Spotify launched a suit against Apple for its unfair advantage of the App Store which would result in a 30% cut of sales of apps sold through the App Store (Vroom et al., 2019). Amazon Prime and Youtube Music both have the advantage of bundling their streaming service with Amazon and Google’s core business services. With many challenges and uncertainties, Spotify adds a podcasting service that may or may not disrupt the music industry as a whole.
